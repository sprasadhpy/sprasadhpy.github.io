Spectrum of Portfolios

Source Code

    1)Efficient Frontier: Set of investment portfolios that yield the highest returns for a specified risk level; 2)Maximum Sharpe ratio PF: Portfolio that offers the highest expected return per unit of volatility or risk; 3)Minimum Variance PF: Portfolio to achieve the lowest possible risk; 4)Kelly PF: Portfolio that satisfies Kelly criterion, allocating capital in proportions that maximize expected logarithmic utility; 5)Risk parity PF: Allocates capital based on the risk contribution of each asset and not return; 6)Equal weighted PF: Each asset within the portfolio is allocated the same proportion of the total investment

Shyaam Prasadh is currently building LLM-powered, multi-agent trading systems for buy-side institutions. He is one of the first Machine Learning hires at Veer,an Oxford spin-off founded by Sir Nigel Shadbolt and Sue Douglas where he works on autonomous multi-agent systems for algorithmic trading and financial chatbots reporting to Prof. Max Van Kleek (Oxford).

Previously, Shyaam held quantitative roles across Trexquant and Elan Capital. At Trexquant Investments, he designed ML-based equity signals that consistently outperformed benchmarks and improved Sharpe ratios. At Elan Capital, he modeled sovereign yield curves using generative models and built an intelligent, LLM-based parser for high-frequency trading datasets.

His academic foundation includes a Ph.D. in Computational Finance from IIT Madras and an MSc in Machine Learning (with Distinction) from University College London, where he was awarded the prestigious Google DeepMind Fellowship. He currently serves as research staff at UCL Robotics exploring how reinforcement learning and LLMs can empower robots with high-level planning, natural language understanding, and cooperative behavior. His work has led to the development of benchmark environments, open-source codebases, and research papers currently under review.

At Ford Motor AI team, Shyaam led a team to build ML evaluation frameworks for auto loan portfolios and developed interpretable risk models aligned with AI safety principles. His work was showcased at the Ford AI/ML Conference in Palo Alto where Andrew Ng delivered the keynote address.

Shyaam’s core expertise lies in deep learning, reinforcement learning, multi-agent systems, post-training LLMs, and algorithmic trading. He has been recognized with the Google DeepMind Fellowship, the Endeavour Research Fellowship by the Australian government, the CRISIL Best Doctoral Research Award, and a UK Global Talent Visa endorsed by the Royal Academy of Engineering.

He is proficient in Python, SQL, C++, and SAS, and experienced with cloud platforms like Google Cloud, BigQuery, Vertex AI, TPUs, and GKE. His core ML stack includes PyTorch and TensorFlow, with acceleration and tuning frameworks such as DeepSpeed, Ray Tune, Optuna, and Hyperopt. In the finance domain, he uses tools like Pyfolio, Riskfolio, PyMC3, and Pycox. For reinforcement learning and multi-agent systems, he works with RLlib, Ray, and crewAI. He has hands-on experience with vision-language and LLM models like BERT, GPT, CLIP, BLIP, Flamingo, and LLaVA, and is fluent with orchestration and retrieval frameworks including LangChain, LlamaIndex, Haystack, Amazon Bedrock, FAISS, and Weaviate. His post-training skillset spans instruction tuning, RLHF, DPO, RLVR, LoRA, QLoRA, mixed-precision quantization, knowledge distillation, and retrieval-augmented generation (RAG). He also builds application layers using FastAPI and Streamlit, and handles MLOps and experiment tracking with Docker, Kubernetes, MLflow, Kubeflow, DVC, and Weights & Biases.

    Othello Games Simulation in NBoard

My catalog includes "Openings" and "WipeOuts," a few resulting in remarkable 64-0 victories for the black side.
