  • I am a Machine Learning MSc graduate student and Google DeepMind Scholar at the University College London, specializing in causal inference and visual computing. Previously, I worked as a ML Architect in Ford GDIA's Independent Model Validation team, where I developed and validated Statistical and interpretable Machine learning models for Ford Credit at a global scale. Prior to this role, I served as a Quantitative Researcher at Trexquant Investments LLP (AUM$2B), applying quantitative and ML methods to build optimized equity portfolios in liquid markets.

  • I am a recipient of the prestigious Endeavour research fellowship awarded by the Australian government and CRISIL's Best Doctoral Research Award in Investment Management (AI in Finance-Track). I hold a Ph.D. in Computational Finance from IIT Madras. My research work was supervised by Prof. M. Thenmozhi (IIT Madras). My thesis chapters are published in prestigious journals like European Financial Management, Finance Research Letters, Research in International Business and Finance, Carbon Management and Decision. My dissertation focused on building causal inference models aimed at addressing the longstanding enigma in finance—how to explain merger waves? My Co-authors are Dr.Chinmoy Ghosh (University of Connecticut), Dr.MayHu (RMIT University), Dr.Robert Faff (University of Queensland), Dr.Syed Shams (University of Southern Queensland) and Dr.Veerasamy Sejian (University of Queensland)

  • My recent research is primarily focused on Causal inference modeling, with a particular emphasis on utilizing influential causal libraries such as DoWhy, Causal Impact, EconML, and CausalML. Demonstrated ability to solve complex problems using software such as Python, Stata, SAS, and Mathematica, with expertise in finance libraries (Pyfolio, Riskfolio), ML and DL libraries (PyTorch, Scikit-learn, Keras, TensorFlow, Pandas, NumPy), Explainable NN libraries (GamiNet), CV libraries (OpenCV and Scikit- image) and Survival Libraries (Scikit-survival, lifelines, pycox).

  • I maintain a blog dedicated to the topic of causal inference. I enjoy trekking and volunteering to teach math and computing to tribal girl children through the Tinklesevaa classroom sessions.
